💡Fossil fuel based electricity production vs CO2 emmisions in 2014 💡

Exploration outcomes from scene 2 :

Key observations the user could be expected to interpret from exploring the second scene are summarized as follows.

⚡ All the countries follow the same color convention as in scene 1 for 2014 as is indicated in the color legend.

⚡ Iceland, Norway and Swedan, three of the highest electric power consumers of 2014 as in scene 1 (2014), generate their electricity requirement mainly through non-fossil fuel means as highlighted through an annotation as well. This makes them eco-friendly countries in terms of electricity production. The tooltip pop-ups are created to not overlap any data points in the lower portion of the scatter plot to help the user understand this.

⚡ Qatar is a clear outlier. From Scene 1 it can be noted that Qatar is consistently in the red space due to high electric power consumption. User could now navigate back to scene 1 to ascertain this previously non-obvious fact.

⚡ There is a general trend that high electric power consuming countries have higher CO2 emission per capita. More red dots towards the right.

⚡ Similarly, there is a trend that low electric power consuming countries have lower CO2 emission per capita. More green dots towards the left.

⚡ Green dots positioned ON the x-axis mostly have missing data and can be seen through the tooltip.
